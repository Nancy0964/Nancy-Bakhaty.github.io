3D CAD Projects

A record of my Fusion 360 CAD work in Design Engineering. Each project below builds on multiple references with my own design decisions. Click on a project title to find out more.

Intro

This is a conveyor belt 3D CAD design done on Fusion 360 for industrial use. It can be used on its own, work with other tools, or have a robotic arm around it to move objects from the conveyor belt. It is X long and X wide with possible height variations between X and X. Most of the design structure and outline was copied from the YouTube tutorial video ‘Solidworks Tutorial # 232 Belt Conveyor Design Assembly and Motion Study by SW Easy Design’ so credits to SOLIDWORKS EASY DESIGN channel. Some modifications were made such as the legs and stands of the conveyor belt. With the help of MacMaster’s 3D designs for parts they sell, having foldable and extending legs was possible. The X was used to implement the foldable and extendable legs for the conveyor. This modification of the conveyor belt makes the device foldable for easy storage since the first tutorial had a fixed bench, and that is not feasible. The type and shape of the aluminum extrusion used were also different for strength and more surface area. The max weight this conveyor can handle is

From a professional and technical perspective, the objectives I set for myself when I started this project were:

  • Advance my skills in Fusion 360 and learn the tools within the software
  • 3D CAD modelling by designing a practical application as an exercise
  • Work on an already existing industry idea and modify things around it rather than invent a new product
  • Compare the work I did with an AI-generated model
